‚ù∂ What is a cost?

A cost is the value of resources used by a business to produce goods or provide services.

Businesses need to understand their costs so they can set prices, control spending, and make informed decisions.

Examples of common costs:
  • Materials
  • Wages
  • Rent

‚ù∑ Direct Costs

Direct costs are costs that can be linked clearly and directly to a specific product, service, or job.

These costs are incurred because the product or job is being made.

Examples of direct costs:

  • Wages of staff working directly on a job

‚ù∏ Indirect Costs

Indirect costs are costs that support the business but cannot be traced to one specific product or job.

üè≠
Examples of indirect costs:
  • Rent for the building
  • Electricity for the premises
  • Salaries of supervisors or administrative staff
  • Depreciation of machinery
üßä
Indirect costs usually stay the same regardless of how many products are made.

‚ùπ Why does the distinction matter?

It is important for a business to know whether a cost is direct or indirect because:

  • Direct costs help calculate the cost of producing each product or job
  • Indirect costs need to be allocated or spread across products
  • Accurate cost classification supports pricing decisions
  • It helps businesses understand profitability and prepare budgets

Understanding this difference is essential for basic costing methods used in accounting.
